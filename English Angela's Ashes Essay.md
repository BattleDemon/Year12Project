
McCourt creates an ironic distance between the pure and unopinionated perspective of a child narrator and the more worldly interpretation of the reader, leading them to address and criticize the world that McCourt has constructed. Because the themes of needless hardship, forced gender roles, and the illusion of moral authority are represented through the eyes of a child, it allows the reader to come to their own grim conclusions about the story of Angela’s Ashes.
